    Step One - The Idea

The first step to making your dream project a reality is to have an idea.
Likely, you already had this before even hearing about Publisher's Nexus.
Take this time to really consider what you'd like your project to look like and what audience you want to reach.

    Step Two - The Call

Most projects can't be done by yourself.
Even if you already have the skills, there's still the matter of time and equipment.
Don't feel the need to do everything yourself.
Reach out to those who might be interested in helping.

    Step Three - The Plan

With your new collaborators, discuss the method.
Plan out your project; look at your assets versus your vision.
Consider your timeline.
Consider your potential pitfalls.
Plan accordingly.

    Step Four - The People

Now find your participants and your audience.
The people you found during Step Two (The Call) are going to be your moderators and managers; they're going to help guide the project to your vision.
The people you connect with here are going to be helping you with the actual legpower to getting there.
Writers, artists, and editors sign on at this stage.

    Step Five - The Content

Here's the bulk of the work: getting your content.
Words must be written.
Art must be made.
Revisions, corrections, and all the creative arguments that follow must happen.
Be patient.

   Step Six - The Assembly

Once the pieces are made, it's time to put them together.
Make sure you check in with your managers and moderators to ensure everything has gone according to plan thus far.
If you didn't invite an editor or layout artist on the project, hopefully your production company can provide one.

    Step Seven - The Production

*Print Only

Unless you have the equipment and supplies to manufacture your final product yourself, this step is largely out of your hands.
Turn your project over to the person or business who will finalize the product and cross your fingers.

    Step Eight - The Purchase

*Optional

If your project is being sold or accepting donations, make sure you advertise well in advance (perhaps during the various calling stages).
Don't wait until Step Eight to think about your financials; those things can get pretty tricky and very messy.
Keep your records clearly.

    Step Nine - The Distribution

Project produced and requested by the audience, it's time to give them what they want.
Deliver it however best accomodates your plan, either digital or physical.

Congratulations! You've created, compiled, and distributed a project!
